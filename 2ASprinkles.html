MYSTERIES OF THE BERMUDA TRIANGLE

Q&A by the 2A Dancing Donuts - Sprinkles

1
Why is it called the Bermuda triangle?
We don't know! It's an area of the Atlantic Ocean between Bermuda, Miami, and San Juan that makes a big triangle shape. Some call it the Devil's Triangle. Not everyone agrees on the limits.
2
Has anyone survived the Bermuda triangle?
Yes - some of us in the room have, lots of planes and ships survive every day.
3
Why do things go missing in the Bermuda Triangle?
Rogue waves, which can be 100+ feet, are a theory. Other theories could be magnetic force or sea creatures or aliens, but we don't have evidence.
4
Why are people afraid of the Bermuda Triangle?
We don't know unless we can talk to an expert who studies fear, but people read false information and have heard stories about people going missing. They might worry that their family could get hurt.
5
Why is the Bermuda Triangle famous?
It has a name, so it's a recognized place where ships go missing. Newspapers are more likely to report on it. Really, ships and planes go missing (rarely) in many places - not more often in the Bermuda Triangle than in similar parts of the ocean.
